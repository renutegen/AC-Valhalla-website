Game Tracking

Eivor Wolf-Kissed (born 847) was a legendary Norwegian Viking warrior, explorer, and ruler of the late 9th century AD.

Did you know that...

  1. Born to the Raven Clan of Fornburg in the petty kingdom of Rogaland, she was orphaned at a young age and adopted by King Styrbjorn of Rogaland, growing up as a younger sister to Sigurd Styrbjornson.
  2. In 872, endless warfare and dwindling resources in Norway motivated Eivor and Sigurd to lead the Raven Clan in a migration from southern Norway to the former Anglo-Saxon kingdom of Mercia in central England, founding the settlement of Ravensthorpe along the River Nene (in present-day Northamptonshire) in early 873.
  3. Eivor ruled the settlement during Sigurd's frequent absences, and he built alliances with both Saxon and Danish rulers across England with the objective of pacifying the island and making it safe for Norse settlement; among his most powerful allies were the sons of Ragnarr Lodbrok and the renowned warlord Guthrum.
  4. Eivor distinguished herself as a kingmaker who helped to install Ceolwulf II of Mercia, Oswald of East Anglia, and Vili Hemmingsson of Hellirborg on their thrones, while deposing rival rulers such as Burghred of Mercia and Ricsige of Northumbria. In 877, she became Jarl of Ravensthorpe after Sigurd voluntarily abdicated in his favor and relegated himself to an advisory role in the settlement.
  5. In January 878, Eivor completed her pacification of England by joining Guthrum's invasion of Wessex - the last of the Anglo-Saxon kingdoms - and driving King Alfred the Great into exile at the costly Battle of Chippenham. Ravensthorpe would continue to grow and prosper under Eivor's rule, and her later adventures took her to Ireland and Francia, during which she participated in the 885-886 Siege of Paris. She ultimately emigrated to Vinland in North America following Wessex's unification of England, and she died and was buried in present-day Massachusetts in the United States.
